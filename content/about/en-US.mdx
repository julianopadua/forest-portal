<AboutSection title="Why Instituto Forest exists" contentWidth="wide">
  Instituto Forest is built on the belief that **technology, data, and engineering** can and should contribute directly to economic, social, and environmental challenges. Beyond that, we aim to join the open source community and contribute directly to our users’ work, education, and access to data.

  Many people still see computing and the environment as very separate fields. In practice, however, environmental issues involve ecology, climate, and land use, but also economics, public policy, and social dynamics. These dimensions intertwine. Ignoring that interdependence often yields fragile, short-lived solutions.

  Drawing on **systems thinking** and the **General Systems Theory** paradigm, we work with the idea that computing, data science, automation, monitoring, and machine learning can support conservation when embedded in well-framed environmental questions.

  Nature is already a complex system. When we add all of the factors above, complexity grows far beyond what a linear reading suggests. Environmental issues therefore demand **integrated, interdisciplinary solutions**, combining method, data, and field monitoring.
</AboutSection>
