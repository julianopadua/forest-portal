<AboutSection title="Why Forest Institute exists">
  Forest Institute is built on the belief that **technology, data, and engineering** can directly help answer real environmental challenges — not as decoration, but as part of the method.

  Many people still see computing and the environment as distant fields. In practice, however, environmental issues are **complex, interconnected systems**: ecology, climate, land use, economics, policy, and social dynamics co-evolve. Ignoring that interdependence often yields fragile solutions.

  Drawing on **systems thinking** and references such as general systems theory, we work with the idea that computing, data science, automation, monitoring, and machine learning can support conservation when embedded in well-framed environmental questions.

  Nature is already a complex system. When we add human, economic, climatic, and territorial factors, complexity grows. Environmental problems therefore demand **integrated, interdisciplinary solutions** — combining method, data, and attention to place.
</AboutSection>
