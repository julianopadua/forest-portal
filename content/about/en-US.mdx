<AboutSection title="Why Instituto Forest exists" contentWidth="wide">
  Instituto Forest is built on the belief that **technology, data, and engineering** can directly help answer real environmental challenges. Beyond that, we aim to join the open source community and contribute directly to our users’ work, education, and access to data.

  Many people still see computing and the environment as distant fields. In practice, however, environmental issues are **complex, interconnected systems**: ecology, climate, land use, economics, policy, and social dynamics co-evolve. Ignoring that interdependence often yields fragile, short-lived solutions.

  Drawing on **systems thinking** and the general systems paradigm, we work with the idea that computing, data science, automation, monitoring, and machine learning can support conservation when embedded in well-framed environmental questions.

  Nature is already a complex system. When we add human, economic, climatic, and territorial factors, complexity grows far beyond what a linear reading suggests. Environmental issues therefore demand **integrated, interdisciplinary solutions**, combining method, data, and field monitoring.
</AboutSection>
