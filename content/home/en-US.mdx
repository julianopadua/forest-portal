<AboutSection title="Institutional purpose" contentWidth="wide">
  Our approach is grounded in the idea that open data must be easily accessible to everyone. The Institute was created to reduce the workload of analysts and researchers, who too often find themselves surrounded by different, scattered data sources.

  The purpose, therefore, is to provide a single centralized environment where users can download data from different sources with the convenience of a unified portal. The Institute is built on an aggregation system that redirects users directly to the original download URLs. In other words, when using the platform, users obtain data directly from primary sources, without having to visit each website individually.

  **General Systems Theory**, proposed by Ludwig von Bertalanffy from the 1940s to the 1960s, guides this Institute, as it enables a systemic view even over data that, at first glance, may seem unrelated. Providing data on financial markets and on fires in the Cerrado might not seem connected, right? That is exactly where Bertalanffy disagrees. A large fire in a coffee-growing region can severely disrupt the coffee futures market. This consequence reverberates directly to producers and market participants, but also generates uncertainties that propagate across many other production chains in the country, affecting investments, capital allocations, and fund returns. It affects far more than can be explained in a short text.

  For this reason, we organize **open, structured, and auditable data**, with process transparency, from collection and validation to final transformation. This way, analysts and researchers who seek to apply a systemic view in their daily work can rely on a centralized data portal.
</AboutSection>
